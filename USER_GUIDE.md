# USER_GUIDE.md

## 1. What This App Is

### 1.1 Who this app is for

This app is for individuals who want to trade stocks or ETFs (funds that track markets) in a disciplined, structured way — especially beginners who want guidance and guardrails.

### 1.2 What problem it solves

Most people lose money trading because they:

* enter trades impulsively
* don’t define risk ahead of time
* exit based on emotion

This app prevents those mistakes.

### 1.3 What this app does **not** do

* It does not predict the future
* It does not automatically trade for you
* It does not guarantee profit

### 1.4 The core idea

Instead of trying to “pick winners,” this app helps you:

> Make structured, disciplined decisions and avoid common mistakes

## 2. Before You Begin

### 2.1 What is trading?

Trading means buying and selling assets (like stocks) with the goal of making a profit from price changes.

### 2.2 Investing vs trading

* Investing: long-term (months to years)
* Trading: shorter-term (days to weeks)

This app is for **short- to medium-term trading**.

### 2.3 What is swing trading?

Swing trading means holding positions for a few days to a few weeks to capture price “swings.”

### 2.4 Risks

* You can lose money
* Prices move unpredictably
* Emotional decisions often cause losses

### 2.5 Mindset

This app assumes:

* patience
* discipline
* willingness to follow rules

---

## 3. Key Trading Concepts Explained Simply

* **Asset**: something you can trade (e.g., stock)
* **Ticker**: short symbol (e.g., AAPL)
* **Entry**: price where you buy
* **Exit**: price where you sell
* **Stop loss**: price where you exit to limit loss
* **Target price**: where you plan to take profit
* **Trend**: general direction of price
* **Pullback**: temporary drop during an uptrend
* **Support**: price area where buyers step in
* **Risk/Reward**: how much you risk vs how much you could gain
* **Position sizing**: how much of an asset you buy

## 4. How This App Thinks

### 4.1 Philosophy

The app prioritizes:

* avoiding bad trades
* enforcing discipline

### 4.2 Why trades are blocked

Blocking trades prevents:

* impulsive entries
* poor risk setups

### 4.3 VALID / WAIT / INVALID

* **VALID** → you may proceed
* **WAIT** → good idea, wrong timing
* **INVALID** → do not trade

## 7. Pre-Trade Evaluation

### Purpose

Determine if a trade idea is worth considering.

### How to use

* Enter a ticker
* Review system output

### Outcomes

#### VALID

* Setup meets requirements
* You can proceed

#### WAIT

* Setup is good, but timing is wrong
* You must wait

#### INVALID

* Do not trade

---

## 8. Actionable WAIT Plans

A WAIT plan tells you:

* where to enter
* when it becomes valid
* what not to do

Example:

* Preferred entry zone: $180–$185
* Do not chase above: $187
* Recheck: when price returns to zone

---

## 9. Trade Builder

You must define:

* Entry zone
* Stop loss
* Target
* Time horizon
* Thesis

This forces you to fully plan the trade.

---

## 10. Trade Validation

### Purpose

Ensure the trade is well-constructed.

### Hard failures

* missing fields
* bad risk/reward
* invalid setup

### Soft warnings

* weaker setups

If validation fails:

> Do not take the trade

---

## 11. Position Sizing

### Purpose

Determine how much to buy.

### Inputs

* account size
* risk %
* entry price
* stop loss

### Output

* number of shares
* capital required

### Key idea

You only risk a small portion of your account per trade.
